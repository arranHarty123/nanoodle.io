Representatives


Decentralise Nano

Transactions on the Nano network are voted on to ensure they are legitimate and protect the network against bad actors who may wish to conduct malicious actions such as interupting transactions and conducting double spending attacks. Voting power is related to the number of Nano that are represented in the vote.

Voting is conducted by a Nano node for its associated representatives. The Nano node software can be run by anybody wishing to help decentralise the network. See here for details on how to run a Nano node.

Nano nodes are associated with 1 or more accounts, all of which can be nominated as a representative by another individual's accounts; this is a sensible approach where an individual is not running a node themselves (so they wouldn't be able to represent the voting power of the Nano in their own accounts). A representative will vote on behalf of all the Nano in all the accounts that have nominated the representative.

Selecting a representative is an important way to secure the health of the Nano network and ensure your Nano voting power is counted. It is an important feature for decentralisation of Nano voting power, and allow's individuals to change their representative at any time for no cost. A representative may be assessed on several factors including:

  • Online Availability
  • Reputation
  • Diversity
  • Version
  • Processing Power
  • Voting Weight
  • Synchronization

In many cases, individuals will keep a default representative on their account. This may be because they score highly on several fronts or will hold their Nano on an exchange where they may not get a choice of representative. This can result in a more centralised Nano network. The long term aim is to have a wider selection of good representatives for an individual to choose from so that Nano becomes more decentralised.

For further reading on the protocol, see the NanoTools wiki
